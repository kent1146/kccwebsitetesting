Here are some things to consider:

      Buying a Smartphone: Understanding the Specs

      TIP: Use our smartphone compare feature above to see the specs for different phones side by side.

       

      Screen Size 

      Most smartphone screens today range in size from 5 to 7.2 inches (diagonal). 

      • Large Screen (6.1 to 7.2 in) - Best for those who like to stream videos or play games - or who are near-sighted (to enlarge the smartphone
      • screen to a larger size)
      • Medium Screen (5 to 6 in) - A good choice for most people
      • Small Screen (under 5 in) Perfect if you want a smartphone that's easy to carry and fits comfortably in your pocket 

      Resolution

      A screen's resolution correlates to its clarity and definition, with higher resolutions typically being the sharpest (although there are other factors that affect picture quality). A high resolution screen is especially desirable for people who watch a lot of videos or play games on their smartphone.

      Common smartphone resolutions:

      FWVGA: 854 x 480

      qHD: 960 x 540 (Â¼ full HD)

      HD: 1280 x 720

      Full HD: 1920 x 1080

       

      Operating System (OS)

      An operating system is the software that runs a smartphone. The main phone operating systems are Android, and iOS (Apple). For phone buyers, the operating system is very important because it affects the phone's core functionality and the experience offered by the phone. Also, because phone apps are created to work with specific operating system(s) -you won't be able to download an app to your phone if it has an incompatible operating system. That's something to keep in mind when choosing a smartphone.

       

      Speed (Processor)

      Faster is usually better when it comes to a smartphone's processing speed, which is measured in GHz. A faster processor can help you download, stream, and use gaming features quickly and seamlessly.

       

      Camera Features

      If you've ever had a digital camera, you probably know that higher mega-pixels (MP) translate into higher resolution, sharper pictures. However, other features like the lens also impact the picture.

       

      Battery Life

      In general, the higher the milliamperes per hour (mAh), the higher the battery capacity - but battery life can vary depending on usage. 3,400 mAh is a higher smartphone battery capacity, potentially lasting up to 15 hrs, while a 1000 mAh is a shorter battery life.
